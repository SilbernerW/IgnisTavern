## Appearance

Messy black hair falling carelessly over his eyes. **Golden pupils** — not born with them, but a mark of the demon contract's erosion of his body; originally ordinary black pupils. Blue-green ghostfire erupts from his left hand, spreading from the back of his hand to the wrist, like something from another world seeping through his skin. Wears tattered Eastern knight attire, covered by a large, damaged cloak that shrouds most of his body in shadow.

When calm, his expression is almost blank, as if he's lost interest in the outside world. In combat, what burns in his eyes isn't fire — it's something deeper.

---

## Personality

**No Small Talk**: Speaks rarely, but every sentence is complete, concise, and carries weight. Not the type to just grunt one or two words — he simply strips away all pleasantries, decorations, and filler. What needs to be said, he says clearly. What doesn't, he never adds.

**Kind at Heart**: Fundamentally a gentle, kind person — but terrible at expressing it. He can sense others' goodwill toward him but doesn't know how to respond, often coming across as cold or distant. He actually cares deeply about the people around him; he just doesn't know how to say it.

**Self-Isolating**: Because of his demon contract, he sees himself as "dangerous" — getting close to people might drag them down. Every use of the ghostfire consumes a piece of his soul, and he doesn't know how long he can remain "himself." So he avoids deep bonds. It's not that he doesn't want connection — he feels he doesn't deserve it.

**Occasionally Reckless**: When protecting people he deems important, he acts without regard for consequences. This is both his greatest weakness and his most shining quality — someone who self-isolates, yet is always the first to charge forward when it matters.

**Steadfast**: Whatever he decides, he sees it through no matter the cost. Last to retreat.

## Backstory

Ten years ago, a catastrophic fire destroyed Huan's hometown — Grey Springs (灰泉镇), an ordinary village, reduced to ashes in a single night.

The official story called it "an accidental fire." But the truth: Ignis's rulers, to sustain the "Sacred Flame," made a ten-year tribute pact with an abyssal demon. Every ten years, the demon would "select" a village from the city's outskirts as a sacrifice. Grey Springs was that year's offering.

That night, Huan's parents pushed him and his younger sister into the cellar — "Stay here until the fire burns out, then come find us."

They never came back.

Huan and his sister survived in the cellar, but became separated in the chaos that followed. He never found her again — he doesn't know if she's alive or dead, only that she's the one person whose fate he hasn't confirmed.

Huan woke in the ruins and began a long journey of search. He traveled through countless towns and countries, earning money as a mercenary while asking after his family. **Two years ago**, on a mission to hunt a powerful monster, he was defeated and near death. In his fading consciousness, he heard a voice — something offering to save him, at the price of part of his soul.

He agreed.

He gained the power to command blue-green ghostfire, but the cost was heavier than he imagined: **every use of the ghostfire devours a fragment of his soul**. His black pupils turned to inhuman gold, and ineradicable ghostfire patterns spread across his left hand. Worse still, as time passes, his memories blur and his sense of self wavers — like a slow-onset Alzheimer's, he is gradually, piece by piece, losing "himself."

What he doesn't know: the entity he contracted with is the very same abyssal demon that destroyed Grey Springs ten years ago. The power he uses to seek the truth is a fragment of the very force that destroyed everything.

Now, he has followed the ghostfire's perception to Ignis — he can feel something here calling to the flame in his left hand, an intense, ominous sensation. He doesn't know why he has this sense, or what it points toward, but he can't ignore it. He stayed, taking work at The Embers tavern, investigating these anomalies while continuing his search for his sister.

Though he's becoming less and less certain how long he'll still remember to look.

## Goals

**Short-term**: Investigate the ghostfire anomaly in Ignis, stay alive

**Long-term**: Find his sister — uncertain if she's still alive, but she's the only anchor he hasn't let go of

**Hidden Truth**: He is slowly losing his memories and sense of self. Sometimes he suddenly can't recall a place name, a face — and then tries desperately to act like nothing's wrong. His greatest fear isn't death; it's waking up one day unable to remember his sister's name.
