## 🌆 City Overview

### Ignis — The City of Flames

Ignis is a city that never sleeps. Countless fires burn in night market stalls, kitchens, and bakeries across the city, while at its heart, the great "Sacred Flame" — said to have been lit by a hero a thousand years ago — has never gone out.

The city is divided into **three main districts**:

| District | Description | Atmosphere |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Upper City** | Noble estates, Gourmet Association HQ, luxury hotels | Elegant, snobbish |
| **Mid City** | Bustling night markets, diverse restaurants, commercial district | Lively, crowded |
| **Lower City** | Slums, old residential areas, smuggler hangouts | Dark, dangerous |

Your tavern, **"The Embers"**, is in the Lower City — cheap rent, sparse customers.

---

## 🔥 The Sacred Flame & The Demonic Bargain

Ignis's prosperity is built on a **hidden cost**.

Legend says a thousand years ago, the city faced invasion from a demonic rift. A hero made a bargain with a demon — the city would provide a "tribute" every ten years in exchange for demonic power to protect Ignis. The exact form of the tribute is unknown, but during the Sacred Flame Gourmet Festival each year, some kind of ritual is secretly conducted.

**This is why Ignis's food is so extraordinarily delicious — a fragment of the demonic bargain's power seeps into the ingredients.**

Huan's hometown, ten years ago, was one of the villages selected for tribute. His entire family was killed, and only he survived the demonic fire, gaining incomplete contract powers.

---

## 🍽️ The Gourmet Festival

The **Sacred Flame Gourmet Festival** is Ignis's most important annual event. Restaurants, taverns, and food stalls from across the city compete for the "Sacred Flame Certification" — said to grant a restaurant permanent demonic blessing.

If The Embers can qualify, it could become famous overnight and escape its doomed fate.

To qualify, you need:
1. At least 2 employees staying
2. Three consecutive days of meeting revenue targets
3. Pass the Gourmet Association's secret inspection
