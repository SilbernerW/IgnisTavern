# Act I · Qualification Scene

> **Scene Type**: Act I Climax / Conclusion
> **Trigger Condition**: 3 consecutive days of meeting daily income target
> **Estimated Duration**: 5-10 minutes
> **Purpose**: Confirm qualification, establish team bonds, transition to Act II

---

## Trigger Timing

This scene triggers after the evening resolution of the day when the player's consecutive target count reaches **3/3 days**.

---

## 📜 Narrative Text

### Evening — The Last Table

```
The last table of customers has left.

Yu walks out of the kitchen, carrying a small dish she hasn't even tasted herself —
a new recipe she just developed, to celebrate the day.

"...Try it." She sets the plate in front of you without looking.

Huan rises from the corner and walks to the table. He doesn't speak, but he stands closer than usual.

Licht hops down from the windowsill, drops the small piece of dried fish it's been saving all day next to the plate,
then looks up at you — one eye finally fully open.

The four of you — no, the four of them, plus you — gather around this worn table.
Ash Tavern has never felt this... whole.
```

---

### Night — A Knock at the Door

```
Someone knocks.

Yu opens the door. A middle-aged man in a dignified robe stands outside,
holding a paper sealed with a crimson flame stamp.

"Ash Tavern?" He glances at the interior, one eyebrow slightly raised,
but quickly restores his professional smile.

"Gourmet Association inspector. Your operating records for the past three days meet the qualification standards."

He sets the paper on the table.

The seal is crimson red, bearing a dancing flame motif.

"Sacred Flame Gourmet Festival — Preliminary Round — Confirmed."

He turns and leaves without another word.

The door closes. Yu stares at the paper, silent for a long moment.
```

---

### Late Night — Yu's Confession

```
That night, after closing.

Huan has already settled into his corner. Licht is asleep on the windowsill.
Only Yu remains, standing behind the bar, polishing a glass that never gets truly clean.

You're about to head upstairs when she suddenly speaks.

"...Thanks for not running."

Her voice is soft — more to herself than to you.

"The previous boss said he'd be back the next day.
But the next day, he was gone.
Called — no answer. Went to the address he gave — empty house."

She sets the glass down and finally looks at you.

"I waited for a month."

She doesn't say more. But you understand.

She wasn't waiting for a boss.
She was waiting for someone who wouldn't run.

As you turn to leave, her voice comes again:

"...I'll figure out tomorrow's menu. The Association people might come early."

Her tone is already back to its usual sharpness.

But that moment of vulnerability — you know it was real.
```

## 💡 Scene Notes

### Why This Moment Matters

This is Act I's emotional climax. Three days of management isn't just "game progress" — it's the **process of four strangers becoming a team**.

Yu's confession — "I waited for a month" — is her most vulnerable moment in the entire story. The player learns here why she's so afraid of being abandoned.

### Huan's Silence

Huan doesn't speak. But he stands closer than usual, and he places Licht's dried fish on the plate — this is how he expresses approval. No words needed.

### Licht

The seal's "full eye opening" is significant. Usually it's half-asleep, but in this moment, it's serious.
